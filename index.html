01 - Mindset

The Mindset Shift

Tools matter, but the best analysts are known for judgment, curiosity, communication, and business understanding.

Curiosity First

Ask "why" before "what". The best analysts are driven by questions, not just queries.

Business Acumen

Data without context is noise. Understand the business before the dataset.

Storytelling > Stats

Your insight is only as powerful as your ability to communicate it clearly.

Continuous Learning

The tools change. Your commitment to learning is the real competitive edge.

"The goal is to turn data into information, and information into insight."
Carly Fiorina

03 - Thinking

Analytical Thinking Framework

A simple repeatable path for going from vague questions to decision-ready insights.

1

Define the Question

What business problem are we solving? Who needs this answer?

2

Gather & Validate Data

Source the right data. Check quality, completeness, and bias.

3

Explore & Analyze

EDA first. Let patterns emerge before jumping to conclusions.

4

Interpret & Synthesize

What does this mean for the business? What is the "so what"?

5

Communicate & Act

Tailor your output to the audience. Drive decisions, not just reports.

Great analysts cycle through these steps repeatedly. Analysis is rarely linear.

04 - Career

Career Strategy

Progression is about depth of impact, not just years of experience.

Entry
Junior Analyst
  • Excel & SQL proficiency
  • Learn Power BI / Tableau
  • Build a portfolio with 3-5 projects
  • Certifications from Google or Microsoft
Mid
Analyst / Sr. Analyst
  • Python for automation
  • Advanced dashboarding
  • Stakeholder management
  • Industry domain expertise
Lead
Lead / Principal Analyst
  • Strategy & roadmap ownership
  • Cross-functional influence
  • Mentoring junior analysts
  • ML & predictive modeling

05 - Visibility

Building Your Brand

Your skills should be visible before an opportunity arrives. Make your work easy to discover, inspect, and trust.

Build in Public

Share projects on GitHub, LinkedIn, and X. Document your learning journey openly.

Niche Down

Pick an industry such as fintech, health, education, or development, and build depth in that space.

Create Content

Write articles, record walkthroughs, post dashboard screenshots, and teach what you know.

Network Strategically

Attend meetups, conferences, and communities like DataFest Africa. Be present online.

06 - Plan

90-Day Data Analyst Roadmap

Use this as a focused learning path. Each phase should leave you with a skill and a project you can show.

Days 1-12

Step 1 - Excel / Spreadsheets

  • Master data cleaning & analysis in Excel
  • Learn pivot tables, VLOOKUP, and Power Query
  • Build a sales dashboard or expense tracker
Project: Personal expense dashboard
Days 13-15

Step 2 - Basic Statistics

  • Mean, median, mode, and standard deviation
  • Distributions & probability basics
  • Apply statistics to real datasets
Foundation for everything else
Days 16-35

Step 3 - Power BI / Tableau

  • Build interactive dashboards
  • Visualize COVID-19 or public datasets
  • Pursue Microsoft Power BI certification
Project: COVID-19 trend dashboard
Days 36-55

Step 4 - SQL

  • Query and manipulate databases
  • Practice joins, aggregations, and subqueries
  • Analyze customer behavior data
Project: E-commerce customer analysis
Days 56-75

Step 5 - Python

  • Use Pandas and NumPy for data analysis
  • Create charts with Matplotlib / Seaborn
  • Clean messy datasets from Kaggle
Project: Kaggle dataset analysis
Days 76-90

Step 6 - Portfolio & Launch

  • Host projects on GitHub
  • Complete free virtual internships through Forage
  • Apply to roles & reach out to analysts
